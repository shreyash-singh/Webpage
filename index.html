About Me

Who Am I?

My name is Suyash Singh. I am a third-year Undergraduate at the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur, pursuing Bachelor of Technology focused on Mechanical Engineering with a minors in Control Systems (Electrical Engineering). I have a deep appreciation for and interest in mechanical engineering and related domains. Having done a good amount of coursework and experienced a bit of research work in the field, I just have nothing but gratitude to be able to realize solutions to very general unseen problems, I couldn't have even thought about!

As of now, I am working as a Research Asoociate in the Machine Tool Dynamics Laboratory, IIT Kanpur under the supervision of Dr. Mohit Law, Assistant Professor at IIT Kanpur. My research work in primarily based on Active-damping of Machine tool vibrations using an Electro-hydraulic actuator. The project involves modelling the actuator, performing its system identification, characterising its dynamics and further designing a controller to operate the system in closed-loop operation. [If you wish to know more, kindly contact me]

Apart from this, I have varied interests ranging from design and fabrication of mechanical components and mechanisms, to actuation and control of mechatronic systems. I am fascinated by the elegant mathematical analysis of complex mechanics problems like those of modelling deformations in solids or designing robotic mechanisms or formulating their kinematic equations. Everything aside, I also love to code, develop software applications and contribute to the Open Source. I like to take up challenging tasks, work out problems outside of my domain and aim to explore and learn much of everything that comes my way.

I tend to spend a good time with my family and friends, watch movies and pen down my views sometimes. I love to travel (on rails), get my photographs clicked (:D) and listen to good songs.

So here I am, learning stuff (!), beyond imagination (!), every day (!) and exploring what all is still left to be !! :)

Dr. Mohit Law, Machine Tool Dynamics Laboratory, IIT Kanpur

  • Setup Design: Designed and fabricated a rigid bracket to mount the actuator such that the piston dynamics remain isolated and uninfluenced from the setup. Research Project: Investigations on the force control of an Electrohydraulic Exciter

5th-25th Dec. 2018

Dr. Mohit Law, Machine Tool Dynamics Laboratory, IIT Kanpur

  • The objective of the project was to study the force control of an EHE in the simulation environment. An Electro-Hydraulic Exciter (EHE) is commonly used for precise control of displacement in machines as well as providing large excitation forces to structures. Starting with basic literature review, two EHE models were implemented and simulated using the Simulink modeling software by MathWorks Inc.
  • The project work provided guidelines to decide upon workable parameters for real-time experiments with an EHE. Open-loop vs closed-loop performance of the model was studied using the simulation environment and appropriate Proportional Integral (PI) feedback control strategy was implemented.
  • A model-based system identification was carried out for given experimental data to find out various design parameters like mass, piston area, chamber volume and flow coefficients for the linear electro-hydaulic actuator.
  • Upon simulation, the frequency response of the output force was obtained and PI tuning and sensitivity analysis were performed using MATLAB. The frequency response plots of the simulated model had a close fit with the experimentally obtained plots.
  • Also, the percentage error in the -3dB frequency of both simulated and experimental bode plots was close to 3%, which further validated the model.
